Guest Recommendations

Explain the points, one by one

1. Research Title
• Research title.
• Supervisor's name.
• Team members' names.
2. Preface
• A brief introduction to the project (why you chose it and why it's important).
3. Acknowledgements
• A brief thank-you to the supervisor, university, or anyone who helped.
4. Abstract
• A summary of the project (project idea, goal, scope, and expected results).
• Keywords: Keywords such as: Fall Prediction, Sensors, Machine Learning, Healthcare.
5. Introduction
• Research Overview Statement: A general definition of the idea.
• Goals and Objectives: The goals you want to achieve.
• Scope: The boundaries of the research (what it includes and what it doesn't).
• Limitations & Constraints: Difficulties or challenges (such as time, resources, or lack of data).
• Assumptions: Your assumptions (e.g., the user always wears the bracelet).
6. PREVIOUS WORKS / LITERATURE REVIEW / CASE STUDY / BACKGROUND
• Previous research in the same field.
• A simple comparison between them and your project.
7. FEASIBILITY STUDY
• Purpose: The goal of the study.
• Justification: Why your project is important.
• Economic Feasibility: Are the expected costs reasonable?
• Technical Feasibility: Is the hardware/software you need available?
• Desired System Functionality: The functions you want the system to perform.
8. RESEARCH PLAN
• Work Breakdown Structure: Dividing the work into phases.
• Activity and Task List: A schedule of tasks for each member.
• Gantt Chart: A graph showing the timeline.
9. SRS – SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS SPECIFICATION
• System Requirements Analysis: System requirements analysis.
• Hardware Requirements: Hardware specifications (e.g., ESP32 + Pulse Sensor).
• Software Requirements: Software specifications (Arduino IDE, Python).
• Other Requirements: Any additional requirements (e.g., SMS API).
10. SYSTEM DESIGN

• Existing Methods: Current methods for solving the problem.
• Proposed Research Methodology: Your method (simple rules or thresholds).
• Proposed Framework: A diagram showing how the system works from sensing → processing → alerting.

11. CONCLUSION (Expected Result)

• What do you expect to come out of the project? (A prototype system that predicts crashes and sends alerts).

12. REFERENCES

• References (previous research + any scientific source or book)
